4. Calculation Flow

Step 1: Base Incentive = Revenue Achieved × Slab Rate
Step 2: Input Bonus = Base Incentive × Input Bonus %
Step 3: Penalty A = Base Incentive × Missed-Connect Penalty %
Step 4: Penalty B = Shortfall Multiplier × Shortfall Amount × Slab Rate (only if below 100%)
Step 5: Subtotal = Base + Input Bonus − Penalty A − Penalty B
Step 6: Penalty C = Subtotal × Closer Penalty %
Final: Payout = Max(0, Subtotal − Penalty C)

Incentive Policy Document

Why This Policy?

This incentive plan is designed to drive the right behaviors at the Team Lead level — not just revenue, but the daily actions that consistently produce revenue over time.

A Team Lead’s job is not to close deals personally. It is to ensure their team’s SDRs are making enough calls, booking enough sessions, and connecting with prospects fast enough — while their closers are consistently hitting individual targets. Revenue is the outcome; the inputs are what a TL can actually control.

The plan rewards both dimensions: output (revenue achieved) and input (SDR activity and operational discipline). It also penalizes gaps in leadership — missed prospect connections, revenue shortfalls, and systemic closer underperformance — because these are the problems a Team Lead is specifically hired to prevent.

1. Philosophy: Output + Input

Most incentive plans only pay on revenue. This plan intentionally splits the incentive into output criteria (60% weight) and input criteria (20% weight), with the remaining 20% determined by penalty avoidance.

  • Output (Revenue) — The team’s total revenue against target. This is what the business needs. Slab-based rates ensure that hitting 120%+ is disproportionately rewarded (2.0% vs 1.25% at 100%), creating a strong pull toward overachievement.
  • Input (SDR Activity) — Talk time, call volume, and sessions booked. These are leading indicators. A team that hits these daily will almost certainly hit revenue targets. The +20% bonus rewards TLs who manage the process, not just the scoreboard.

2. Why Slab-Based Rates?

Flat commission rates create no urgency to push past target. With slabs, a TL earning 1.25% at 105% has a clear financial reason to push to 120% where the rate jumps to 2.0%. The jump from 1.25% to 2.0% at the 120% threshold is the single biggest accelerator in the plan.

Below 70%, the incentive is zero. This sets a minimum performance floor — the company cannot subsidize teams that deliver less than 70% of their target.

3. Why Penalties?

Penalties exist because a Team Lead’s job includes preventing specific failures:

  • Penalty A (Missed 16-hour Connect) — Speed-to-lead is one of the highest-leverage activities in sales. Research consistently shows that contacting a prospect within the first hour increases conversion rates by 7x compared to contacting them after 24 hours. The 16-hour SLA is generous. Missing it repeatedly signals a process breakdown that the TL is responsible for fixing.
  • Penalty B (Revenue Shortfall) — A proportional deduction that scales with the size of the miss. This prevents a scenario where a TL at 92% of target earns nearly the same as one at 99%. The closer you are, the less you lose.
  • Penalty C (Closers Missing Targets) — If all 5 closers miss their individual targets, that’s not 5 individual problems — it’s a coaching and management problem. The -25% penalty for all closers missing is the strongest signal in the plan: your job is to develop your people, not just inherit results.

5. What Good Looks Like

A Team Lead who consistently:

  • Hits 100%+ of team revenue target
  • Ensures SDR talk time, calls, and sessions are all at or above minimum daily thresholds
  • Keeps missed connects below 3 per month
  • Has no more than 1–2 closers missing individual targets

…will earn their full base incentive + 20% input bonus with zero penalties. At Rs 50L target and 110% achievement, that’s approximately Rs 82,500/month in incentive.

6. What Bad Looks Like

A Team Lead who:

  • Hits only 76% of target (70–89% slab: 0.75%)
  • Misses input metrics (no bonus)
  • Has 12+ missed connects (-15% penalty)
  • All 5 closers miss individual targets (-25% penalty)

…will earn a heavily reduced incentive. At Rs 50L target and 76% achievement, that could be as low as Rs 19,000/month after all penalties stack.

7. Guardrails

  • Minimum floor: Incentive can never go negative. Floor is Rs 0.
  • Payment cycle: Incentives paid in the following month’s salary.
  • Session definition: A session = a scheduled, attended demo/consultation with a qualified prospect. No-shows do not count.
  • 16-hour SLA: Measured from lead assignment timestamp in CRM to first outbound call/WhatsApp/email timestamp.
  • Closer targets: Defined separately per closer based on experience tier and territory.
