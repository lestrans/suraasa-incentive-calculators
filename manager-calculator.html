5. Calculation Flow

Step 1: Base Incentive = Revenue × Slab Rate
Step 2: Input Bonus = Base × (20% | 10% | 0%)
Step 3: Kicker = (Revenue − Target × 1.2) × 0.75% (only if >120%)
Step 4: Penalty A = Base × Missed-Connect Rate
Step 5: Penalty B = Shortfall Multiplier × Shortfall × Slab Rate
Step 6: Subtotal = Base + Input + Kicker − PenA − PenB
Step 7: Penalty C = Subtotal × TL-Failure Rate
Step 8: Penalty D = (Subtotal − PenC) × Closer-Failure Rate
Final: Payout = Max(0, Subtotal − PenC − PenD)

Incentive Policy Document

Why This Policy?

A Manager’s job is to build and sustain a high-performing sales organization — not to close deals or even manage individual SDRs directly. This incentive plan is designed to reward Managers for the outcomes that only they can influence: aggregate revenue performance, operational consistency across multiple teams, and the development of Team Leads who can independently deliver results.

The plan combines output incentives (revenue slabs), input monitoring (SDR activity at aggregate level), an overachievement kicker (exclusive to the Manager role), and a layered penalty system that holds Managers accountable for both Team Lead performance and systemic closer health.

The kicker exists at the Manager level because Managers are the ones who set stretch targets, allocate resources across teams, and create the conditions for overachievement. When the entire organization exceeds 120% of target, the Manager has earned the right to participate in that upside without a cap.

1. Philosophy: Aggregate Outcomes + Leadership Accountability

Unlike Team Leads who are measured on a single team, Managers are measured on the aggregate performance of all teams. This means:

  • One underperforming team can be offset by a strong team — but only up to a point.
  • If most Team Leads are missing their targets, the Manager is penalized regardless of aggregate numbers. This prevents the scenario where one star TL masks systemic leadership failure.
  • If most closers across all teams are missing targets, that’s an organizational problem the Manager owns.

2. Why Lower Base Rates Than Team Leads?

Manager base rates (0.5% – 1.25%) are lower than TL rates (0.75% – 2.0%) because the Manager’s revenue base is much larger. A Manager overseeing 3 teams with a Rs 1.5Cr aggregate target earns on the full Rs 1.5Cr — 3x the revenue base of any single TL. The lower rate on a larger base produces comparable or higher absolute payouts.

3. Why the 120%+ Kicker?

The overachievement kicker is exclusive to Managers because:

  • Managers set the ceiling. They decide hiring plans, lead allocation, training investments, and team composition. When the whole org overperforms, the Manager created those conditions.
  • The 120% threshold is intentionally high. This is not a participation trophy — it requires every team to meaningfully exceed target. The kicker at 0.75% of revenue above 120% rewards truly exceptional months without diluting the incentive for normal performance.
  • It’s uncapped. If aggregate hits 150%, the Manager keeps earning. This aligns Manager incentives with company growth: there is never a point where the Manager stops caring about more revenue.

4. Why Four Penalties?

Managers have more penalty layers than TLs because they have more levers of control:

PenaltyWhat It CatchesWhy It Matters
A: Missed ConnectsAggregate SLA breaches across all teamsThe Manager sets the operational cadence. If connects are being missed across teams, the process is broken at the org level.
B: Revenue ShortfallProportional miss against aggregate targetSame as TL — scales with the size of the miss.
C: TL FailureHow many Team Leads missed their targetsIf all TLs miss, the Manager failed to coach, escalate, or reallocate. If all-but-one miss, there’s still a systemic problem even if aggregate looks okay.
D: Closer Failure% of all closers missing individual targetsIf >60% of closers across the org are missing targets, hiring, training, or lead quality is broken — all Manager-level decisions.

6. What Good Looks Like

A Manager who consistently:

  • Hits 110%+ aggregate revenue
  • Ensures all 3 input metrics are met across all teams
  • Keeps total missed connects below 6
  • Has all Team Leads at or above their individual team targets
  • Has fewer than 40% of closers missing individual targets

…will earn full base incentive + 20% input bonus with zero penalties. At Rs 1.5Cr target and 110% achievement, that’s approximately Rs 1,98,000/month.

Push to 140% and add the kicker: approximately Rs 3,37,500/month.

7. What Bad Looks Like

A Manager who:

  • Hits only 72% aggregate (70–89% slab: 0.5%)
  • Misses input metrics (no bonus)
  • Has 43 missed connects across teams (-15%)
  • All 3 TLs miss targets (-25%)
  • 93% of closers miss targets (-15%)

…will see incentive reduced to approximately Rs 19,000/month after all penalties stack.

8. Guardrails

  • Minimum floor: Incentive can never go negative. Floor is Rs 0.
  • Payment cycle: Incentives paid in the following month’s salary.
  • Session definition: A session = a scheduled, attended demo/consultation with a qualified prospect. No-shows do not count.
  • 16-hour SLA: Measured from lead assignment timestamp in CRM to first outbound call/WhatsApp/email timestamp.
  • Closer targets: Defined separately per closer based on experience tier and territory.
  • TL targets: Each Team Lead has a pre-set team target; the Manager is evaluated on how many TLs hit theirs.
